Zayre’s SPH Tutorial (18+ ONLY)

  1. Make sure to buy the right zipper! The best kinds to get are the thick, plastic toothed, non-separating zipper manufactured by YKK. It’s recommended to NOT use the zippers you can find at places like Walmart or Michaels since they’re not as durable and run the risk of catching in longer pile fur or jamming! Highly recommend checking out WAWAK which carries a good selection of these zippers at a good price! https://www.wawak.com/zippers/jacket/molded-plastic/ykk-5-molded-plastic-non-separating-jacket-pocket-zipper (Non-Affiliate Link)
  2. Figure out exactly where you want your zipper positioned. Once your zipper is sewn in, it’s a real pain to make adjustments so try and make sure you’re satisfied with the position BEFORE sewing it in! Put your suit on and mark exactly where you want your zip to start and end. Then, turn your suit inside out and position your zipper on the inside and line it up appropriately.
  3. Decide whether you want your zip pull to face inwards or outwards. Inwards will make your SPH a little more discreet but wearing your suit a little less comfortable (especially if it’s tight-fitting). Facing it outwards will make it easier to open/close without having to de-suit first but has a higher chance of snagging fur. There’s not a ‘right’ position, so pick whichever suits your suiting style better!
  4. Pin your zipper in place, and do your best to keep a consistent tension across the entire zipper. This will ensure that the zip lays flat against the fur when wearing.
  5. If you do not already have a seam where you are placing your SPH, make sure to cut it now. This will allow you to test and make sure your SPH zip is not more obvious than desired.
  6. Time to sew in that zipper! This can be done either by hand-sewing, or machine-sewing. If machine-sewing, an All Purpose thread with a double-stich should do the job however if hand-sewing, a straight stitch with Upholstery thread should do the job. Just be mindful to brush out any fur fibers that get caught in your stitch, which will help hide the zip.
Congrats! You should have successfully added an SPH to your suit!
